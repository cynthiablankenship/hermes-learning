# M01 - AI and ML Foundations

**Status:** 🔵 In Progress
**Prerequisites:** Basic Linux CLI familiarity (you've got this)
**Time estimate:** 5-7 days at 1-2 hours per day

## Why This Module Matters

You troubleshoot the hardware that runs AI workloads. But to troubleshoot effectively, you need to understand what those workloads are actually *doing*. This module gives you the mental models — what a model is, what training means, what inference means, why GPUs matter for this, and what's happening when things go wrong.

No math. No coding. Just concepts.

## What You'll Learn

By the end of this module, you will be able to:
- Explain what AI and machine learning are in plain language
- Understand the difference between training and inference
- Know what a neural network is conceptually (not mathematically)
- Understand what a "model" actually is — a file, a program, a data structure
- Explain why GPUs are used instead of CPUs for AI
- Understand what "parameters" and "weights" mean when people say "70B parameter model"
- Read an AI-related log entry and have a basic idea of what's happening
